Recommended Care Measures

{% if result.risk_level == 'strong' %}

1. Immediate Professional Consultation: It is strongly recommended to consult a healthcare professional immediately for further evaluation and confirmation. An endocrinologist or diabetologist consultation may be beneficial.

2. Blood Glucose Monitoring & Insulin Therapy: Regular blood glucose monitoring should be started immediately. Insulin therapy may be required based on medical advice and confirmatory test results.

3. Strict Dietary Management: A strict, medically supervised diet plan with controlled carbohydrate intake and avoidance of refined sugars should be followed. Consult a registered dietitian for personalized guidance.

4. Physical Activity Modifications: Physical activity should be limited to light or moderate levels until proper medical guidance is received. Exercise should be carefully monitored and adjusted based on blood glucose levels.

5. Psychological Support: Psychological support or professional counseling may help in managing stress, anxiety, and emotional challenges associated with health concerns. Consider reaching out to mental health professionals or support groups.

6. Complication Screening: Early screening for potential complications such as eye (retinopathy), kidney (nephropathy), and nerve damage (neuropathy) should be considered. Regular ophthalmology and nephrology assessments may be needed.

7. Family Awareness & Emergency Preparedness: Family members should be informed about the screening result. Emergency preparedness (glucagon kits, emergency contacts) and continuous monitoring tools (glucometers, continuous glucose monitors, mobile health apps) are essential.

{% elif result.risk_level == 'moderate' %}

1. Medical Consultation & Monitoring: It is advisable to consult a doctor at your earliest convenience for professional guidance. Regular monitoring of blood glucose and HbA1c levels should be scheduled.

2. Balanced Diet with Reduced Sugar: A balanced diet with reduced sugar and refined carbohydrate intake should be maintained. Proper nutrition planning is important for health stabilization.

3. Regular Physical Activity: Regular physical activity such as walking, cycling, or swimming should be incorporated into your daily routine. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week.

4. Stress Management: Stress management techniques such as relaxation exercises, meditation, yoga, or mindfulness should be practiced regularly to support overall well-being.

5. Healthy Lifestyle Routine: Maintaining a healthy lifestyle with proper sleep (7-8 hours), consistent daily routine, and avoidance of harmful habits is beneficial for health stabilization.

6. Basic Complication Screening: Basic screening for potential complications can be considered during your next medical visit, if deemed necessary by your healthcare provider.

7. Health Tracking & Awareness: Awareness of symptoms and simple health tracking using mobile applications can support better monitoring of your health status and help identify any changes.

{% else %}

1. Routine Health Maintenance: Routine annual or biennial medical check-ups are sufficient to maintain overall health and detect any changes early.

2. Continue Healthy Diet: A healthy and balanced diet should be continued as part of daily life. Focus on nutritious whole foods and hydration.

3. Regular Physical Activity: Staying physically active helps in maintaining long-term well-being. Aim for regular exercise as part of your lifestyle.

4. Mental Health Support: Mental health should be supported by maintaining a stress-free lifestyle, engaging in enjoyable activities, and fostering positive social connections.

5. Healthy Daily Habits: Healthy daily habits such as proper sleep (7-8 hours per night), regular physical activity, and mindful eating should be followed consistently.

6. Complication Prevention: There is no immediate need for complication screening at this stage. Continue with general preventive health care practices.

7. Preventive Health Awareness: General health awareness and preventive care practices should be maintained. Stay informed about health trends and maintain regular communication with healthcare providers.
